Consent to Data Collection

In order to perform its function, Carat must periodically collect generic information about your device and how you are using it. This information includes the following:

  • what apps are running,
  • the % battery remaining,
  • memory and cpu utilization,
  • a unique device ID,
  • the battery state (e.g., plugged in), and
  • the OS version and phone model.

We DO NOT collect any personally identifying information: no names, no email addresses, no private data from other apps, and so on. The app uses a low-power feature of Location Services to be notified of significant changes in your location and to take samples. Carat does NOT use GPS (we never record your absolute location) or run in the background, period. Carat has a public github repository, so you don't need to take our word for it.

After collecting data about your device for a week or more, it will give you personalized recommendations. If your battery is running down quickly, it isn't Carat's fault, but we hope it will help you discover the real culprit.

Carat periodically sends this information to our servers, where we do math on it and subsequently share the results with you. Carat tries to do all of this unobtrusively. Although we encourage you to open Carat every now and then so it can send us new data, you can pretty much forget it's running until you'd like to investigate a battery problem.

Carat is an academic research project. We reserve the right to publish our results and to release the data into the public domain. (Remember: the data does not identify you in any way.) We will never sell your data or use it to advertise to you.

We respect your privacy and value your trust. When you downloaded Carat, you agreed to our EULA, which contains additional information. If you have any questions or concerns, just ask.
